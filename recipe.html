Japanese Miso Fish Curry

Serves 3–4

Ingredients

Fish

  • 500 g firm white fish (seer fish, tuna, snapper, or cod)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper

Curry base

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small carrot, diced
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 2 cups water or fish stock
  • 1–2 tbsp Japanese curry powder (or mild curry powder)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey

Miso mixture

  • 2 tbsp white or yellow miso paste
  • 3 tbsp warm water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the fish: Season the fish with salt and pepper, then lightly pan-fry for 1–2 minutes per side until just colored. Remove and set aside.
  2. Make the curry: Heat oil in a pot, sauté the onion until soft, then add garlic, carrot, and potato. Cook for 2–3 minutes, pour in water or stock, and stir in curry powder, soy sauce, and sugar. Simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 10–15 minutes.
  3. Add the miso: Mix the miso paste with warm water until smooth. Turn the heat to low and stir the mixture into the curry. Avoid boiling vigorously after adding miso.
  4. Finish: Gently add the fish and simmer on very low heat for 3–4 minutes, until cooked through.

Serve with

  • Steamed rice
  • Pickled vegetables
  • Spring onions or sesame seeds on top

Helpful note

If you do not have sake, you can use 1 tbsp rice vinegar mixed with 1 tsp sugar, or 1 tbsp mirin, or simply leave it out because the miso and soy sauce already bring plenty of Japanese flavor.

This curry is milder and slightly sweeter than a Sri Lankan fish curry, with a gentle fermented depth from the miso.
